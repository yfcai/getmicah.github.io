Hi, you reached my homepage, where you will find information related to my professional activities.

I am a researcher and PhD candidate at the Unversity of Tübingen. I like finding pragmatic applications for theories in computer science and mathematics. Concretely, I'm using the tools in lambda calculi, denotational semantics and type systems to reason about incremental, generic and probabilistic programming.

If there is anything you'd like to know, please do not hesitate to email me.

Curriculum Vitae

Software Projects

[Tutorial Assignment] This Scala console application scheduled tutorials for "Informatik 1", a 500-student lecture at the university of Tübingen. It integrates with the lecture's forum based on Discourse. Students and tutors enter their time tables as a forum setting; the teaching staff enter the availability of rooms. The software assigns students and tutors to rooms and timeslots such that all tutors have similar numbers of students. The scheduling problem is then encoded as a minimum-cost-flow instance with edge activation constraints, to be solved by Gurobi via Optimus.

[monad-bayes] A probabilistic programming library for Haskell, based on monad transformers. An earlier version is described in this paper by Ścibior, Ghahramani and Gordon. I mainly contributed an implementation of Metropolis-Hastings algorithm.

[CREG] A Scala macro library to generate regular functors from datatype descriptions; essentially a port of deriveTraversable for GHC with fewer restrictions on type-level functions. The experience working on this library eventually led to a paper about equirecursive types in generic programming.

[ILC-Scala] [ILC-Agda] Software artifacts of the incremental lambda calculus project. ILC-Scala implements a simple functional language with a code transformation turning ordinary programs into incremental programs. [ILC-Scala] is a machine-checked proof that the code transformation preserves the meaning of programs.

Publications

[Fω] System f-omega with equirecursive types for datatype-generic programming. By Cai, Giarrusso and Osterman at POPL 2016.

This paper argues for the importance of equirecursive types in generic programming and shows some feasiblity results: type soundness in the presence of higher-order equirecursive types, and decidability of type checking for the first-order fragment.

[ILC] A theory of changes for higher-order languages: incrementalizing λ-calculi by static differentiation. By Cai, Giarrusso, Rendel and Ostermann at PLDI 2014.

The paper produced by the incremental lambda calculus project.

[H-free] Incompressibility of H-Free Edge Modification. By Leizhen Cai and Yufei Cai at IPEC 2013.

This paper characterizes whether H-free edge modification problems have a polynomial kernel when H is a path, cycle or 3-connected graph.
